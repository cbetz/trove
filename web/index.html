How to read this

Method, definitions, caveats

charity_gap = HCRIS S-10 charity care cost − Schedule H 7a financial assistance at cost

Positive gap → the hospital reports more charity care to CMS than to the IRS. Negative → the reverse.

A nonzero gap doesn't automatically mean a hospital is hiding community benefit. Plausible reasons the same hospital reports different numbers on each form:

  • Definitional differences. Schedule H 7a excludes some categories that HCRIS S-10 may include (Medicaid shortfall handling, partial-payment netting, charity vs. uninsured discount classification).
  • Time alignment. Hospital fiscal years often don't match calendar tax years; we use the latest 990 per EIN by tax_period_end, mismatch up to ~12 months is common.
  • Cost-to-charge ratio handling. HCRIS S-10 applies a hospital-wide CCR; Schedule H allows different cost-step-down approaches.
  • Charity care policy boundaries. The same patient case can be classified as charity, bad debt, or contractual allowance — moving cost between lines.

A negative gap is often legitimate when a hospital files Schedule H but doesn't bill Medicare in volume — children's hospitals, specialty cancer centers, and major teaching hospitals with disproportionate non-Medicare populations all show this pattern.

The data is a starting point for review, not a definitive accusation. The value is making the comparison computable at scale.

Coverage: 3,079 of 6,042 HCRIS hospitals appear in the CBI crosswalk; the other ~50% are mostly for-profit and government hospitals (no 990) or hospitals not in CBI's 2024 vintage. Bootstrap pair: HCRIS FY2023 vs. 990 TY2022 from the IRS 2024 release.
