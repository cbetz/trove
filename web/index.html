The first numbers

1,317 nonprofit hospital systems in the U.S. file Worksheet S-10 with CMS and Schedule H with the IRS. Both filings are supposed to report the cost of charity care under different rules. We just made the comparison computable.

  • $58.7B in total community benefit reported on 990 Schedule H for tax year 2022.
  • $5.0B total absolute gap between what the same hospital reports as charity-care cost on its Medicare Cost Report vs. its 990.
  • 61% of systems report more charity care to CMS than to the IRS.
  • Top single gap: Memorial Hermann Kingwood, $428M HCRIS vs. $307M 990 — +$120.8M.

Full table: community_benefit_gap_2022.csv · Top 50: community_benefit_gap_2022_top50.csv · Method and caveats: summary.
